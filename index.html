Installation. 

WALK TO 

THE MOUNTAIN / 3

Rodrigo Isaza

 

The walk starts early, at dawn. When daylight is out, everything is ready. Everything is always ready from the night before because it’s about leaving early. Baskets where the mecto goes and the ingredients for lunch; boxes where drinks go; pots if lunch is sancocho. Luggage for the ride includes, of course, ball, tire for which nothing little, umbrellas because the day will be sunny; crockery with plates and plastic cutlery and tablecloth to extend it to the edge of the lake or in some rest of the mountain where the walk stops to thank the landscape.

Everyone will jump into the water. From the rock as experienced swimmers or little apoco, first one foot, then the other, to check the temperature of the current or the depth of the puddle. Everyone or almost everyone will wear the swimsuit under the t-shirt and shorts. You will enjoy the moment, the blue sky with fleeting clouds, the company because the walk unites. The nudity that is transparent in the glare of the water is mixed with the nudity of nature that houses, away from urban noises and makes the moment and the place a Paradise, like the one painted by Hyeronimus Bosch in the first panel of "The garden of delights" where naked bodies mingle with the unique nature that surrounds and exalts them.

Saúl Alvarez Lara
